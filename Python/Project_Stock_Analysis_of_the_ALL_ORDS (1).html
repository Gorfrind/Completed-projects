Stock Analysis of the ALL ORDs

</p>

Abstract¶

The ALL ORDs (all ordinaries) is the top 500 shares of the ASX (Australian Stock Exchange). Shares in the ASX are grouped into the sectors that the companies operate in. This project is a stock analysis of the ALL ORDs index of the ASX which attempts to understand the role sector plays in the ASX as well as to identify top and bottom shares.

As this data is relatively clean the most important part of this project is the identification of which columns play the largest role and how they relate to each other.

Shares in general are a great way to save money and an easy way to diversify savings. This project will attempt to identify the shares that are best suited for different types of investment. Additionally, this project will drill down on sector in an attempt to understand how sector affects a shares performance.

1. Motivation¶

The share market is hard to predict as there are many factors that influence it, some completely unpredictable (including scandels). Many people attempt to understand and make prediction on the share market and thus this project will attempt to find understanding that others have not or at the very least further educate investment decisions.

An example of recent events that have affected major shares are the royal commission and the election. Specifically looking at NAB (National Australia Bank), the share price of it initially fell (after the royal commission) and then rose (after Liberal were elected). Between this period there was a decent profit to be made. NAB can also be looked at as a good passive share (or dividend/yield stock) and these movement would generally not affect people that bought it as such. NAB it is a company that pays a full franking (tax free), reasonably high dividend, thus a good place to invest (like with all the major banks on the ASX).

Another example that my project could not account for is the recent upturn, in the share market, of rare metal companies. Rare metals have been a very strong export (as Australia is one of the few places to export Cobolt as an example) and are expected to rise further as Trumps trade war continues, forcing foreign countries look to other places to obtain there metals. Recent events like this have given a boost to rare metal companies, however as my project is not a historical analysis it would not pick up on sudden trends like this and it is hard to find a database that can be used to account for current events.

This project however should be able to identify trends like NAB when it was lowest being a strong investment or at least further educate someone looking at buying a share.

2. The data¶

The first dataset used come from FactSet, FactSet Research Systems provides computer-based financial data and analysis. It consolidates data on global markets, public and private companies, and equity and fixed-income portfolios. (databases are not listed on factset website thus dataset is included with this project)

The second dataset used comes from Market Index, a financial portal for the Australian stock market. It provides up-to-date ASX market analysis, share prices, charts and index performance data in an easy-to-digest format and links to easy-to-use datasets. https://www.asx.com.au/asx/research/ASXListedCompanies.csv (the second dataset used)

EV is best looked at as a factor of a companies performance compared to sector. If a company has an EV at 4 times the sector EV it is either performing exceptionally well or is overvalued, likewise if it is below the sector EV it is either performing badly or undervalued. Thus, looking for low EV ones and a knowledge of the company is necessary to determine where it lies.

Insights gained from sector:¶

Looking overall¶
  • Financials is the most important Sector by weighting (most shares compared to price)

  • Lesser sectors like Consumer Discretionary, Health Care and Information Technology are expected to have the most shares grow by eps and thus are good for investment

Looking at dividends¶
  • Financials has the highest average dividend payments, however, the average growth (by yield) is comparatively low. This makes sense with financials being the sector with the banks and having the highest weighting has the highest average
  • Once again the sectors Consumer Discretionary, Health Care and Information Technology are expected to have the most shares grow by dividends despite them having a low mean growth
  • In general it seems that dividends that decrease are expected to decrease by far more than increasing dividendss will increase by
  • Materials has about an even number of expected increasing and decreasing dividends while the mean dividend increase is low and the mean decrease is high


Insights gained About shares:¶

Attempting to realise a gain¶
  • It is possible to identify plenty of shares under their sectors EV. These shares will either be undervalued or the companies are just poorly performing (qualitative analysis required)
  • Linking EV to other values seems to give a random spread of shares (specifically linking to the P/E, though there was generally more gain than loss)
  • There are many other factors that can be limited in these shares in an attempt to find only undervalued shares though it is still random
Growth and defensive shares¶
  • These types of shares are easily identifiable
  • Industry standard to find top shares are through dividend growth and eps growth. These shares could be called the top shares of the ALL ORDs
  • Dividend yield can be much higher for smaller riskier companies
  • Some larger companies will pay the taxes on their dividend payments and thus they will be tax free, thus unlike interest earnings or sale earnings will not be taxed.
